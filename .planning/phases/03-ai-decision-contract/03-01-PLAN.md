<threat_model>
- Threat: free-form model text bypasses the schema boundary and leaks directly into execution. Mitigation: require the model to emit only the `ai-decision-output.schema.json` payload and reject anything else.
- Threat: invalid or uncertain outputs get silently repaired or coerced into executable actions. Mitigation: the protocol and `brain-node` contract must fail closed and preserve the blocked record for audit.
- Threat: prompt, schema, and model versions are not recorded, breaking replayability. Mitigation: the persisted decision record must require `prompt_version`, `schema_version`, and `model_label`.
- Threat: the persisted decision record becomes a dumping ground for execution logic. Mitigation: keep the record limited to input summary, validated decision, and validation metadata only.
</threat_model>
